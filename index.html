My internship in Ministery of Innovation and Technology

March 10, 2026

During my 45-day summer internship at the Ministry of Innovation and Technology, I collaborated with a cross-functional team to build a web platform for university students, teachers, and the global community. Our project featured a resource library for educational materials and an opportunities board for internships and scholarships. I worked on the [front-end/back-end] team, using React, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS (or Node.js, Express, and MongoDB). We followed agile practices with daily stand-ups, Trello, and Git for version control. By the end of the internship, we delivered a functional prototype that the Ministry plans to continue developing. This experience taught me real-world collaboration, agile workflows, and the importance of user-centered design. I'm proud to have contributed to technology that empowers education and connects communities.

My first Hackathon Experience

March 15, 2026

I also participated in my university's first-ever hackathon, **"AGT HUB"** at Gibi Gubae in Adama Science and Technology University. Our project focused on **digitalizing church services** to make them more accessible and efficient. We built a platform with features like user registration, secure login, anonymous question submission, online donations, and attendance tracking. This solution aimed to modernize church operations while maintaining privacy and community connection. Competing in this hackathon pushed me to think creatively, collaborate under tight deadlines, and present a working prototype to judges. It was an unforgettable experience that boosted my confidence and passion for building tech with real-world impact.
